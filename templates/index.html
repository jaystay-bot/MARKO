Jay Cheat Sheet

What everything means (click to expand)

Campaign

A named bucket of leads you're working. Tie one campaign to one offer (e.g. "BookerMove – Movers VA"). You can have many campaigns going at once but only one is ACTIVE — new scrapes/leads attach to it.

Niche

The business type you're targeting (movers, roofers, med spas, etc.). Pick from the preset list or type your own. The scraper searches DuckDuckGo for "{niche} in {city}, {state}".

Lead Status

NEW = scraped, never contacted. SENT/CONTACTED = email went out. RETRY = transient SMTP failure (421/450/451/452) — will auto-reset to NEW after cooldown. FAILED = permanent failure or {{ max_retries }} retries used up. REPLIED = manual flag.

Email Templates

Reusable subject + body with merge fields like {{ "{{business_name}}" }}, {{ "{{city}}" }}, {{ "{{owner}}" }}. Click "Preview" on any template to see exactly what gets sent. Single-brace syntax also works.

Call vs Email

Lead has email → cold email first (cheap, scalable, fast feedback). Phone-only lead → call. If a lead has both, email gives you context for the call.

Retry

A RETRY lead waits {{ cooldown_minutes }} minutes (cooldown), then "Reset Eligible" flips it back to NEW so the next batch will try again. After {{ max_retries }} retries it auto-FAILS.

Dry Run

Send Batch with "dry run" checked = goes through every step except the SMTP call. Marks leads CONTACTED locally so you can preview the workflow without burning real outreach.

Daily Cap

Hard limit of {{ daily_cap }} real sends/day. Today's count comes from the activity log. When the cap hits, /send refuses with a BLOCKED message. Dry-runs bypass.
